My Advocacy in spreading awareness towards the importance of dog adoption in the philippines

Why Adopt

In 2012, the Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) have recorded 100 cases of animal cruelty in the country and declared to receive an average of 40 calls daily with regards to reports on animal abuse and neglection. One of these reports was the violent and brutal maltreatment of a drunk owner of the dog Wacku, whose snout was mercilessly gashed off with a machete. The very same case rapidly disseminated across the globe and caught the attention of many. Fortunately, along with the warm love it received from the countless feedbacks, a director of a non-profit dog rescue organization Liesl Wilhardt, brought the survivor from the Philippines to the US after it was adopted.


In another story from another year, a non-profit no-kill animal shelter in the southern Philippines called Happy Animals Club, spread and served as encouragement for dog lovers and enthusiasts all over the word. The noticeable motive for the inspiration gathered from this organization was from the fact that it is ran and started by a 9-year-old (now 15) boy. The genuine effort of the little boy's pure intentions has caused many people from different countries to donate and help the club grow and stay active.


This advocacy aims to use my voice as a tool in educating and spreading awareness towards animal cruelty and pet neglect between dogs in the Philippines as a way to encourage dog adoption.

Philippine Animal Welfare Society

Is one of the prominent non-profit organizations in the Philippines dedicated in protecting and defending the welfare and well-being, as well as promoting appropriate treatment of all animals. PAWS also offer other services such as low cost spay or neuter (Kapon), Trap-neuter-return (TNR), adoption, animal rescue and rehab, shelter cleaning and feeding program. Spay is a verb referring to the removal of ovaries of a female animal, and neuter is for the testicles of a males. Both of which are done to reduce overpopulation and diseases from unwanted animals/pets. Trap-neuter-return or TNR is a practice done by animal welfare organizations in controlling the population of stray cats in various communities.

Animal Kingdom Foundation

The Animal Kingdom Foundation or AKF is another vital part of animal rescue, protection and conservation of animals in the country, along with PAWS. AKF have shown a more active approach in fulfilling their roles, this involves the collaboration with the Philippine National Police or PNP in raiding illegal slaughterhouses and establishments that contain unlawful activities such as dog trading and dog meat selling.

Philippine Animal Rescue Team

The Philippine Animal Rescue Team (PART) stands out as a significant force for animal welfare in the Philippines, being recognized as the first and only no-kill, SEC-registered animal rescue organization in the country. Comprised of dedicated volunteers committed to transforming the treatment of animals both locally and globally, PART relies on the kindness and generosity of its members and animal lovers worldwide to support their mission. Emphasizing the importance of compassion, responsible pet guardianship, and education, PART believes in instilling these values at the individual and community levels. Their commitment extends to advocating for spaying and neutering, proper animal care, and stricter punishments for those guilty of animal cruelty. With a mission to rescue abandoned, sick, and injured animals, provide quality veterinary care, and secure forever loving homes, PART envisions a broader societal change by raising awareness about animal cruelty and abuse. The organization's core values center on kindness to animals, community involvement, and responsible leadership, reflecting a holistic approach towards fostering a compassionate society for both humans and animals.

CARA Welfare Philippines

Compassion and Responsibility for Animals (CARA) Welfare Philippines, established in 2000, is a non-profit, non-government organization committed to improving the conditions of animals in the Philippines. Operating without government funding, CARA relies solely on private donations to support its endeavors. The organization envisions a compassionate Philippines where all cats and dogs have loving homes and has outlined a comprehensive mission to achieve this goal. CARA focuses on promoting animal welfare through education, public awareness, affordable sterilization, veterinary care, and the rehabilitation and rehoming of cats and dogs in need.

11 Signs of Animal Abuse, Neglect, or Cruelty

1

Physical Injuries

Visible wounds, cuts, or bruises on the animal's body without a reasonable explanation.

2

Emaciation

Severe thinness or emaciation, indicating malnutrition and lack of proper care.

3

Lack of Shelter

Animals left outside without adequate shelter from extreme weather conditions, such as heat, cold, or rain.

4

Inadequate Living Conditions

Living spaces that are excessively dirty, cramped, or lacking proper ventilation.

5

Lack of Food and Water

No access to clean water or proper nutrition, resulting in dehydration or starvation.

6

Tethering

Tying or chaining an animal for extended periods, restricting its movement and access to necessities.

7

Social Isolation

Animals that are consistently isolated from human or animal interaction, potentially indicating neglect.

8

Visible Signs of Fear or Aggression

Animals displaying extreme fear, aggression, or submissive behavior in the presence of their owners.

9

Unsanitary Conditions

Accumulation of feces, urine, or other waste in the living area, posing a risk to the animal's health.

10

Lack of Veterinary Care

Untreated medical conditions, injuries, or infections that have not been addressed by a veterinarian.

11

Unusual Behavior

Drastic changes in behavior, such as constant cowering, excessive barking, or unusual aggression, which may indicate distress.

Cruelty to Animals

Animal Hoarding. Animal hoarding is a practice of keeping a significant number of animals as household pets without adequate necessities such as housing space, food, and proper care. People who deny these inadequacies and or are showing signs of compulsive hoarding are stated to be characterized as symptoms of mental disorders. According to The Humane Society of the United States, animal hoarders are likely able to impose severe neglect on animals and therefore are unintentionally enacting animal abuse.

Cruelty to Animals

Animal Neglect. Animal neglect refers to the situation where pet owners fail to undertake responsibilities such as supplying sufficient food and water, providing proper shelter, or arranging monthly/yearly (or whatever is preferred) veterinary care. Animal neglect can be done deliberately such as leaving a dog outside as a house guard, or unintentionally such as hoarding. When this activity is extended to a crucial period of time, it can result to detrimental health issues or eventually death.

Cruelty to Animals

Tethering and Caging. An article from Happy Animals Club Titled Animal Abuse in the Philippines, shows a series of pictures taken in just 2 afternoon walks. These images exhibit multiple animals, specifically dogs, whom are either tied to trees, walls, gates, and windows with ropes, or are confined in fixed metal cages. Either way, the visually evident incommodious shelters, such as cages too small for the animal, areas with uncleaned feces, improper shade and hygiene, were clear signs of abuse. In addition, it is said that since tethering and caging dogs are ubiquitous and common in most neighborhoods, especially in rural environments, people have erroneously assume that these activities are appropriate when it fact it is unacceptable and is retracting their freedom of movement. More examples include caging dogs outside as household security, or displaying them as an indication of social status, or keeping them as investment in breeding.
